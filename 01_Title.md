## Abstract

Software development has evolved significantly over the last few decades. Traditional development methodologies such as the Waterfall Model often struggle to accommodate rapidly changing customer requirements and dynamic business environments. As a result, Agile methodologies have become increasingly popular, offering greater flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement.

Among Agile methodologies, Scrum has emerged as one of the most widely adopted frameworks for managing complex projects. Scrum emphasizes iterative development, cross-functional teamwork, continuous customer feedback, and incremental product delivery. Rather than attempting to define every requirement at the beginning of a project, Scrum encourages teams to adapt throughout the development process while continuously delivering business value.

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the Scrum framework. It introduces the history and evolution of Scrum, explains its core principles, describes its roles, events, and artifacts, and illustrates how Scrum operates within modern software development environments. The report also compares Scrum with traditional software development models such as Waterfall, V-Model, and Spiral, highlighting the strengths and limitations of each approach.

Furthermore, the report explores practical applications of Scrum across multiple industries, presents a real-world case study based on Spotify's engineering culture, discusses commonly used Scrum tools, addresses common misconceptions, and examines current trends such as Agile scaling and AI-assisted project management.

The objective of this report is to provide students, software developers, project managers, and engineering professionals with a clear understanding of Scrum and its role in delivering high-quality products through continuous collaboration, adaptability, and customer-focused development.
